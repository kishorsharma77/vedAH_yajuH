9. He who wishes the increase of Brāhmaṇa power shall wear skins only; he who wishes the increase of Kṣatriya power shall wear cloth only; he who wishes the increase of both shall wear both (skin and cloth). Thus says a Brāhmaṇa. [^2] 

[^2]:  See also Gopatha-brāhmaṇa I, 2, 4.
</details>

<details><summary>हरदत्त-टीका</summary>

##### सूत्रम्
ब्रह्मवृद्धिमिच्छन्नजिनान्येव वसीत, क्षत्रवृद्धिमिच्छन् वस्त्राण्येव, उभयवृद्धिमिच्छन्नुभयमिति हि[^१] ब्राह्मणम् ॥ ९॥  
###### प्रस्तावः
'काषायं चैके वस्त्रमुपदिशन्ती' त्यारभ्य वासांस्यजिनानि च विहितानि । तत्र कामवशेन विशेषमाह—  
##### टिप्पनी
ब्रह्मवृद्धिः ब्राह्मणवृद्धिः क्षत्रवृद्धिः क्षत्रियवृद्धिः ॥ ९॥  

[^१]:

    अत्र गोपथब्राह्मणस्य प्रथमप्रपाठकस्य द्वितीया कण्डिका द्रष्टव्या ।

25. Bringing all he obtains to his teacher, he shall go begging with a vessel in the morning and in the evening, (and he may) beg (from everybody) except low-caste people unfit for association (with Āryas) and Abhiśastas. [^9]  

[^9]:  Regarding the explanation of the term Abhiśasta, see below, I, 7, 21, 17. Haradatta: 'Apapātras are called those born from a high-caste mother and a low-caste father, such as washermen. For their cooking vessels &c. are unfit for the use of the four castes. . . . Since Āpastamba says, In the evening and in the morning, food obtained in the evening must not be used for the morning meal, nor food obtained in the morning for the evening meal."' Manu II, 182, 183, 185; Āśv. Gṛ. Sū. I, 22, 4. See also Gopatha-brāhmaṇa I, 2, 6.
</details>

<details><summary>हरदत्त-टीका</summary>

##### सूत्रम्
सर्वं लाभमाहरन् गुरवे सायं प्रातरमत्रेण भिक्षाचर्यं
चरेद्भिक्षमाणोऽन्यत्राऽपपात्रेभ्योऽभिशस्ताच्च ॥२५॥  
##### टिप्पनी
अपपात्राः प्रतिलोमजा रजकादयः। अपगतानि हि तेषां पात्राणि पाकाद्यर्थानि चतुभिर्वर्णैसह । अभिशस्तान् वक्ष्यति 'अथ पतनीयानी'त्यादिना । तानुभयान् वर्जयित्वा अन्यत्र भिक्षेत । तत्र भिक्षमाणस्सर्वं लाभं यञ्च यावञ्च लब्धं गोहिरण्यादि तत्सर्व[^१]ममायया गुरवे आहरेत् । एवमहरहः कुर्वन् सायं प्रातरमत्रेण न हस्तादिना भिक्षाचर्यं भिक्षाचरणं चरेत् कुर्यात् । 'सायं प्रात'रिति वचनान्न सायं गृहीतेन प्रातराशः, नापि प्रातर्गृहीतेन सायमाशः ॥ २५ ॥

<details><summary><h4>सर्वाष् टीकाः ...{Loading}...</h4></summary>
<details><summary>Bühler</summary>

A Brāhmaṇa declares: Since a devout student takes away from women, who refuse (to give him alms, the merit gained) by (Śrauta)-sacrifices, by gifts, (and) by burnt-offerings (offered in the domestic fire), as well as their offspring, their cattle, the sacred learning (of their families), therefore, indeed, (a woman) should not refuse (alms) to the crowd of students; for amongst those (who come to beg), there might be one of that (devout) kind, one who thus (conscientiously) keeps his vow.
</details>

###### प्रस्तावः
